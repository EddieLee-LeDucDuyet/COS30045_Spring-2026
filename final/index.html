From rare to routine: how mobile phones changed enforcement.

Between 2008 and 2024, mobile phone use behind the wheel went from a minor enforcement priority to one of the fastest-growing traffic violation categories in Australia. The introduction of automated camera detection transformed the landscape.

Key Finding

Camera-based detection began in 2019 in select jurisdictions (NSW, QLD, ACT, VIC) and now accounts for over 80% of all mobile phone fines. This represents a fundamental shift from reactive to proactive enforcement.

Demographic Insight

Ages 26–39 and 40–64 account for over 75% of all mobile phone fines, reflecting higher vehicle usage and smartphone adoption in these demographics. Enforcement is heavily concentrated in major cities and inner regional areas, with limited data from remote regions.

Geographic Pattern

NSW leads in absolute numbers, driven by early camera adoption. VIC, QLD, and ACT have also implemented camera programs. States without camera systems (SA, WA, TAS, NT) show steady but much lower enforcement levels relying primarily on police stops.
