Key Finding

Speeding accounts for over 93% of all fines recorded from 2008 to 2024. Camera-based detection dramatically increased enforcement output from 2015 onward. Meanwhile, mobile phone use fines surged as smartphone adoption grew — and camera-based enforcement began in certain states from 2019.

Young Driver Insight

Drivers aged 17–25 show disproportionately high rates of unlicensed driving and seatbelt violations relative to their share of the population. In contrast, the 26–39 and 40–64 age groups dominate raw speeding fine numbers, likely reflecting greater vehicle kilometres travelled.

Population Context

NSW and VIC dominate total fine counts, consistent with their larger populations. However, per-capita rates reveal that some smaller jurisdictions — particularly ACT and NT — have distinct enforcement patterns, with NT showing elevated unlicensed driving and seatbelt offences in remote areas.
